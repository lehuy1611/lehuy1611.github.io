Projects

2022.02-2022.04

Tracked-Vehicle Retrieval by Natural Language Descriptions

The 6th NVIDIA AI City Challenge 2022 @ CVPRW 2022

Our team proposed a robust natural language-based vehicle retrieval system with a new domain adaptive training method which can enhance the model's knowledge and tackle the domain bias problem. In addition, the proposed framework requires less computation capability and data compared to previous top performance methods but still yields a competitive result in AI City Challenge 2022 with top 2 on both the public test set and private test set among total 15 teams.

  • My contributions: Handle all of the models-driven modules in the framework. For retrieval models, I adopted previous top performance methods and improved the retrieval model by modifying the network architecture based on self assumptions from data with various approaches, e.g., adopt better backbone, add new input stream to enhance the robustness of the model, and test new objective losses. In addition, I also proposed a new domain adaptive training method and new format for textual data for multi-modal, specifically text-image retrieval models to address the domain bias between training set and test set. Finally, for classification models, I performed transfer learning by re-using the trained retrieval model and modified its layers to turn it into classification models in order to leverage the trained knowledge of the retrieval model.

2018.05-2019.12

VSight

Highschool Science Fair Project

A smart device that helps visually impaired people in their daily life with three main functions:

  1. Assist the user to move freely and guide them to avoid obstacles while moving indoor and outdoor using Computer Vision and Point Cloud.
  2. Help users find their requested personal belongings and detect objects that are visible in front of them using Deep Learning.
  3. Constantly update the location of the user through GPS and the user can request assistance from their family at their location with just a button using IoT device and connect arduino with python to send the gps signal to online server.
The device had already been tested with blind students at Nguyen Dinh Chieu special high school and received many positive feedbacks. Our solution was able to beat all of other teams in the Vietnam National Youth Informatics Contest 2019 and become the 1st place winner and receive many other city-level awards in Science Fair for highschool students.
